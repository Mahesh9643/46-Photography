"Half Saree - Beauty in Simplicity."




A langa voni is a traditional dress worn mainly in South India by young girls between puberty and marriage. It is also known as the two-piece sari or half sari. It comprises a langa, a skirt which is tied around the waist using string, and a voni, oni, or davani, which is a cloth usually 2 to 2.5 metres (6 ft 7 in to 8 ft 2 in) in length. The voni is draped diagonally over a choli. Usually, the garment is woven with cotton or silk. The modern day "lengha-style sari", worn by Indians across the subcontinent mainly for special occasions, is inspired by the langa voni.
